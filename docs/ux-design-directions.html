wedX – UX Design Directions

High-level layout and interaction directions for the core wedX web app. These act as reference frames for designers implementing high-fidelity mockups.

Direction 1 – Wizard-First OS

Full-screen, low-chrome wizard for first-time setup, followed by a structured dashboard.

  • First session: 5-step cultural wizard (type, events, vibe, rituals, plan).
  • After completion: redirect to “Today” dashboard with tasks seeded from wizard outputs.
  • Ideal when couples are overwhelmed and need strong guidance before seeing a dense UI.

Direction 2 – Three-Column Control Room

Desktop experience that feels like a calm control room rather than a generic admin panel.

  • Left: vertical navigation with Today, Checklist, Events, Guests, Vendors, Budget, AI Planner, Inspiration, Settings.
  • Center: primary content (Today’s tasks, event timeline, checklists).
  • Right: AI Planner, vendor conversations, and inspiration strip.
  • Mirrors mental model from ride-hailing and fintech apps: core actions central, assistance on the side.

Direction 3 – Vendor & WhatsApp Bridge Hub

Focused flows and layouts for combining partner vendors with WhatsApp-based vendors.

  • Two-core tabs: “Find vendors” (partner marketplace) and “My vendors” (booked/shortlisted + WhatsApp leads).
  • WhatsApp flows: simple rows with screenshot uploads, extracted details, and quick actions (“create task”, “attach to budget”).
  • Keeps informal conversations from disappearing while preserving simple mental model for users and developers.

Direction 4 – AI-Coordinated Planning

Chat-based co-pilot tightly wired into events, tasks, vendors, and budget.

  • AI Planner lives in right rail or full-screen mode with deep links into Today, Checklist, Events, Vendors, and Budget.
  • Responses always include structured actions (tasks, notes, changes) rather than generic advice.
  • Couples can ask, “What are we missing for Poruwa?” and immediately see checklists and tasks updated.
